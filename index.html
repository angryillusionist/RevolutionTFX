RevolutionTFX

Power shift.

The AHU that puts you in the driver’s seat.

Choosing a central station air handler unit to match your project needs used to be a grind. This is no longer the case. RevolutionTFX is an AHU with modular configurations to give you a cost-effective solution that’s efficient, reliable and versatile.

Case #1: Corrosion resistance and rust prevention

Potash is a corrosive material that destroys metal through chemical reaction. In potash mines, air-handling units must be replaced frequently, and many only last between three and five years. For a potash mine in Saskatchewan, Canada, RevolutionTFX units were built with special added options. These included stainless steel interior liners, copper tubing and copper fin coils with ElectroFin® coating to help prolong the life of the equipment and protect the owner’s investment.

Energy saving

Air-side economizers save energy by supplying cooler outside air to condition the indoor space. When the outside air is suitably cool and has a lower energy content than the indoor space, the dampers in the mixing box can be controlled in order to take advantage of the lower-energy outdoor air. Economizer configurations include barometric relief, powered exhaust and powered return fans.

So many options

With 34 different segments, RevolutionTFX lets you mix and match cabinet options and accessories. The highly configurable modular central station unit delivers up to 30,000 cfm and can be assembled using a variety of fans, coils, dampers and filter segments, all containing a host of accessories, too.

Footprint

There are several configurations that will deliver the air distribution power you need with a smaller footprint. These include direct drive plenum fans that eliminate the need for straight duct runs and allow a variety of duct-mounting locations. You can also choose a tw0-tier vertical configuration when mechanical room floor space is limited.

Flexibility

The many configuration options available with RevolutionTFX provide you with all the flexibility you need to tailor a unit to match your needs. For example, use a split construction to fit tight spaces such as door, elevator or roof openings, or control the growth and transfer of airborne microbial agents using UV lighting.

Right-sizing

With 36 different sizes, the RevolutionTFX offers choices to fit your space along with a variety of configuration options. You have the power to create an air-handling unit that delivers exactly what you need, not more. The result is an optimized product that does the job you want in the most cost-effective, energy-efficient manner. You don’t have to settle for a unit that’s too small or too big.

Case #2: Quick Ship saves the day

When a contractor needed an AHU in a hurry, the RevolutionTFX Quick Ship program helped them avoid missing occupancy timelines. The modular configuration also gave the contractor the ability to fit the unit in the space without making any building modifications, saving more time and money.
